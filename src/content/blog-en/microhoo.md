If you live on this planet, you'll have heard that last Friday [Microsoft launched a takeover bid for Yahoo!](http://www.nytimes.com/cnet/CNET_2100-1014_3-6228705.html). If we were already stunned when [Google acquired YouTube for over a billion euros](http://www.microsiervos.com/archivo/internet/confirmado-google-compra-youtube.html), this time Microsoft has offered around $44 billion — pocket change, naturally.

It seems Microsoft has concluded that if they want to compete with Google on the internet **they need this acquisition**, and I agree with them (not that I like it). Because — correct me if I'm wrong — they're not the leader in a single online service they offer (not even with [Windows Live Hotmail](http://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Windows_Live_Hotmail) or [Windows Live Messenger](http://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Windows_Live_Messenger)).

[Microsoft had already shown interest in Yahoo! last year](http://www.techcrunch.com/2007/05/04/microsoft-pursues-yahoo-takeover/), but back then Yahoo! wasn't interested. This time the situation is different — Yahoo!'s stock is [at rock bottom](http://finance.yahoo.com/q/bc?s=YHOO&t=2y&l=on&z=m&q=l) and it looks like Redmond is going all in.

In response to the letter Steve Ballmer sent to Yahoo!'s board of directors announcing his intentions, the board declared they would "**carefully consider the offer**". This has triggered some alarms — there are even reports that Mr. Google himself has been in touch with Mr. Yahoo! to try to prevent the sale…

As for me, I won't believe it until I see it — and even then it'll be hard to process — so I'd rather wait before digging deeper into the implications and consequences of what would be **the largest acquisition in the history of the internet**.
